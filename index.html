Who are you?

Hi there! I'm Aretha Walls, a full-stack web developer located in Tucson, Arizona.

I graduated from the University of Arizona in May of 2017 with Bachelor of Arts degrees in Linguistics and Philosophy, with a focus on Ethics. By the time I had graduated, I was confident that a lifetime of academia wasn’t for me.

I began working full-time for a local digital public relations firm, Ridgewood Associates, the day after graduation. My position focused on support at first, but when you work for a very small company, you wind up taking on all kinds of tasks. I started working with Ridgewood’s web-master a few months into the job and found I enjoyed writing the brief snippets of code assigned to me more than I expected.

I had taken a few computer science courses on a whim in college, but coding for the web felt different. I could create usable, visually appealing applications after a few weeks of study—a far cry from Intro to CS, where we were told not to touch GUI libraries until the end of the first semester.

Over the next few months, I became very comfortable writing HTML and CSS, and began dipping my toes in the wide ocean of JavaScript. In the summer of 2018, I learned that Ridgewood would be going out of business in December. I figured it was time to sink or swim and decided to enroll in the University of Arizona’s Coding Bootcamp.

I’m currently neck-deep in an intense, six-month full-stack curriculum and loving it.
